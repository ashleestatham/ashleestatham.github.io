            <h3>

                Why Learning Data Science in Social and Interdisciplinary Contexts Matters

            </h3>

            <p><em>Published: January 2026</em></p>


            <p>

                Learning data science in social and interdisciplinary contexts matters

                because data is not just numbers — it represents people, behaviors, and

                real-world outcomes. Modeling social topics and people allows us to

                better understand patterns that are often invisible at the individual

                level, such as inequality and access to resources. When used correctly,

                data science can have a positive and powerful impact on public policy,

                healthcare, education, and technology.

            </p>


            <p>

                At the same time, working with data about people comes with significant

                responsibilities. Data is rarely neutral, and the way it is collected,

                modeled, and interpreted can sometimes create biases and/or overlook

                important context. As someone learning data science, I often think

                about ethical responsibilities such as protecting privacy, avoiding

                harmful assumptions, and being transparent about limitations in data

                and models. Understanding who is represented — and who is not — is just

                as important as building accurate models.

            </p>


            <p>

                Another reason interdisciplinary learning matters is because social

                problems cannot be solved through technical skills alone. Data science

                benefits from perspectives in sociology, psychology, ethics, and public

                policy. Without these perspectives, it’s easy to misinterpret results

                or apply models in ways that cause harm. For that reason, learning to

                ask thoughtful questions is just as important as learning how to code.

            </p>


            <p>

                Through my DTSC Studio II course, I’m hoping to better understand how

                data science tools can be used to explore social questions responsibly

                and thoughtfully. I want to develop both technical skills and critical

                thinking skills so that I can analyze data in a way that respects the

                people involved. Ultimately, my goal is to use data science not just to

                find patterns, but to contribute to more informed and ethical

                decision-making.

            </p>

        </article>
